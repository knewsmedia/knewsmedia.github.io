Ailing Reggae Entertainer MC Fullstop Gets Financial Support Days After Touching Medical Appeal

Tuesday, November 16, 2021 at 5:39 PM by  Brian Ajon
  • MC John Maina aka MC Fullstop has been hospitalised at Nairobi West Hospital for the past weeks
  • While announcing his illness to his fans on social media, MC Fullstop had appealed for help from people of goodwill
  • A local betting company, Odibet, got wind of his situation and came to his rescue

Popular Kenyan MC John Maina aka MC Fullstop has received financial support after being bedridden for weeks.

The super entertainer has been missing in action due to the ailment.

Via his Instagram, he told his fans was suffering from a critical disease of the lungs known as labor pneumonia.

“My sincere apologies to all fans for not being able to perform on radio, TV, clubs or events, for now, I have been unwell and currently hospitalized (admitted) undergoing treatment for a disease of the lungs – Labor pneumonia at the Nairobi west hospital. I thank everyone for the positive energy and love. God willing, I will be back soon,” read the post.

MC Fullstop discharged from hospital

On Monday, November 15, a betting firm, Odibets, under their Odimtaani initiative stretched a helping hand to him.

The recuperating MC was discharged from the hospital on the same day after his bill estimated to be over half a million was cleared.

"MC Fullstop disclosed that he was suffering from a critical disease. Touched by the reggae MC's plight, we decided to clear his Hospital bill," read part of the firm's update.

Betting Company rescues DJ Lastborn

In August, the Kenya reggae fraternity and Odibets came to the rescue of DJ Lastborn who suffered a stroke for the second time.

The first stroke affected his right side and the second one made him lose his speech.

The two groups agreed to support the turntablist weeks after friends and family appealed to raise medical support for DJ Lastborn.

The betting company also promised to provide upkeep and one years’ rent for the veteran reggae DJ who has entertained people a while back.

The said:

“We decided to support DJ Lastborn who has been ailing for quite some time. As a brand, we are committed to giving back to the community and supporting worthy causes."
